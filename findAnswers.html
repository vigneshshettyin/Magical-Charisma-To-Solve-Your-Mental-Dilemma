Magical Thinking to solve your problems

Magic doesn’t necessarily have to be a physically existing item, rather one can always make magic happen through their philosophies and words. And we strive to being these two seemingly opposing ideas together in the form of magically transforming your lives through our magic cards.

Are you stuck on a problem and cannot find a way out?

Practice the Buddhist philosopher’s trick of remembrance. Rather than asking your mind to search for a solution to a potentially impossible challenge, ask your mind to remember the solution. The presupposition that one once knew the answer creates the mindset that the answer must exist, thus eliminating the crippling conception of hopelessness.

Upset with your reality and do not know what to do with your life?

Always remember that there are many realities, many versions of what may seem obvious. What may appear as the unshakeable truth, its exact opposite may also be true in another context. After all, one’s reality is but perception viewed through various prisms of context.

Obsessed over your phone and cannot stay a moment without it?

We are living in a digital age, and the way we perceive and comprehend our lives has shaped up in different ways over the years. We humans are social beings, we crave interaction and companionship, and the strength of our relations plays a significant role in our emotional and mental well-being. While it is necessary to stay connected and have people whom we can rely on, depending solely on social media for networking can have detrimental effects.

Has social media taken a toll on your mental health?

While scrolling through our feed might feel like a harmless part of your daily lives and a way to pass some free time at hand, we seldom fail to realise that, this harmless activity can affect our well-being in more ways than one.Brush off the temptation of tracking the life of your peers or random people.

Stuck with co workers who exude negativity at your workplace?

The toxicity that a few people exude tends to bring down the morale at a workplace along with negatively affecting the well-being of those who work with them. While it can get tempting to give such people a taste of their own medicine by treating them in a similar manner, it does not work out that way. It is crucial to have the mental and emotional bandwidth to establish boundaries and set limits from toxic people.

Dealing with money problems and cannot make ends meet?

It’s good to have money and the things that money can buy, but it’s good, too, to check up once in a while and make sure that you haven’t lost the things that money can’t buy. Always remember that there is a gigantic difference between being rich and earning money, so stop chasing money and focus on leading a beautiful full life.
